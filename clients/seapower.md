# Client: Seapower

Related: [[The Script]] [[Memory Loop]]

Marine surface care products — boat exterior and interior cleaners, waxes, polishes, protectants. Founded 1970s by TR Industries. USA manufacturing (South Gate, California).

---

## BUYER PROFILE: "Captain Dave" Mitchell

**Demographics:** 48–62, primarily male (female boat owners growing), coastal/lake communities (FL, TX, MI, NC, CA, TN, MN, SC). Household income $85K–$225K+. Business owner, contractor, manager, or skilled tradesperson. Married with kids or empty nesters.

**Core Identity:** "I'm not just a boat owner. I'm a captain. This boat is a reflection of who I am."

**Boat Ownership Story:** Dave worked hard for this boat — it's a trophy representing decades of sacrifice. Whether $28K pontoon or $140K center console, it's proof of success. His peers notice it. His family bonds on it. But maintenance is becoming overwhelming and embarrassing.

---

## THE CORE PROBLEM

Boat owners are overwhelmed by the time, cost, and complexity of maintaining their vessel's appearance. They're juggling multiple products, getting inconsistent results, watching their investment deteriorate — and dreading the maintenance that used to feel like pride.

---

## TOP 5 EMOTIONS (In Order of Power)

1. **Frustration** — "I've spent close to $500 on cleaners over two seasons and my gel coat still looks like hell. What am I doing wrong?"
2. **Wounded Pride** — "That boat is supposed to turn heads. When it pulls into the marina looking dull, that's on ME."
3. **Financial Anxiety** — "Every season it looks worse. I'm watching a six-figure investment depreciate in real time."
4. **Resentment** — "I bought this to enjoy life, not spend every Sunday scrubbing with six different products."
5. **Quiet Inadequacy** — "That guy in slip 14 has a boat worth half of mine and it always looks twice as good. Does he know something I don't?"

---

## TOP 5 BIGGEST FEARS (The 3AM Version)

1. **Irreversible Damage** — "What if oxidation is already too far gone? What if I've permanently damaged the gel coat? How much will that cost to fix professionally?"
2. **Judged at the Marina** — "Everyone notices. A neglected boat tells people you don't have your act together. And mine is telling that story."
3. **Losing Resale Value** — "Buyers hammer you on cosmetics first. I could be leaving $15K–$20K on the table because I couldn't maintain the exterior."
4. **Using Wrong Product / Making It Worse** — "I've already made one mistake on the vinyl. Now I'm scared to touch anything. How do you know what's safe?"
5. **Loss of Identity** — "This boat is proof that I made it. When it looks bad, I feel like I'm losing control of something I was supposed to have handled."

---

## HOW FEARS AFFECT KEY RELATIONSHIPS

| Relationship | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Spouse** | He snaps when she asks if boat is "ready." She suggests hiring someone, which stings his pride. He becomes defensive. |

| **Adult Son** | Kid's passing comment about "chalky hull" lives rent-free in his head. Now hyperaware every family outing. |
| **Financial Friends** | Buddy keeps asking if he's "maintaining that thing properly." He has no confident answer. Shame eats at him. |
| **Social Circle** | He was "the boat guy" who organized trips. Now he's withdrawing. Social capital he worked for is evaporating. |

## HURTFUL THINGS PEOPLE SAY (Intentional or Not)

- **Wife (supportive):** "Maybe just call a detailer? It would be so nice to have it looking right again." → He hears: "You can't handle this."
- **Marina Neighbor (helpful):** "They do details down the dock. Might be worth a call." → Dave replays for three days.
- **Adult Son (curious):** "Dad, what happened to the hull? Is that oxidation?" → Quietly destroys him. He stumbles through an answer.
- **The Know-It-All:** "You gotta be careful with cheap cleaners — some strip UV protection. Looks like you might've damaged it." → Dave has to smile and take it. Goes home and stares at hull for an hour.
- **Financially-Minded Friend:** "You're gonna take a serious bath at resale if the finish isn't right." → Confirms his deepest fear.

## WHAT THEY'VE ALREADY TRIED (And Why It Failed)

| Solution | Result |
|---|---|
| Cabinet full of single-surface bottles | "Can't remember which is which. My garage looks like a marine store aisle and nothing is actually working." |
| Big-box products (Star Brite, Meguiar's) | "Takes forever, results don't last, half a Sunday gone, back is shot. Two weeks later it looks the same." |
| Professional detailer (paid $450) | "Looked incredible for a month. Can't justify monthly. When it faded, almost felt worse — now I KNOW what it can look like." |
| YouTube DIY Rabbit Holes | "Two-hour research, bought buffer and compound, spent Saturday on it. Some results. Not sure I did it right. $200 equipment barely used." |
| Boating Forums & Facebook Groups | "Got 40 different answers. Everyone swears by something different. Still don't know who to trust." |

---

## WHAT THEY REFUSE TO DO

- **Don't want to spend weekends cleaning instead of boating** — "I didn't buy this boat to maintain it. I bought it to BE on the water."
- **Don't want another shelf of specialized products** — "One thing. ONE product that actually handles everything. That's what I want."
- **Don't want to pay professional every month** — "$400 every month all season? Not realistic. I should handle my own boat."
- **Don't want to risk damaging surfaces experimenting** — "I've already made one mistake. I need something SAFE across the board."
- **Don't want to become a marine detailing expert** — "I'm busy. Just tell me what to use. I shouldn't have to deep dive to clean my boat."

## THE MAGIC GENIE TRANSFORMATION

### If a Genie Could Snap Their Fingers:

1. **One product. Every surface. Done.** — "Grab it, run it across every surface, looks showroom fresh. No switching, no second-guessing, no wasted time."
2. **Compliments become routine again** — "I want the 'nice boat' comments back. People slowing their wake for a second look. My wife seeing I've got it handled."
3. **Protection that actually lasts** — "Clean it right, know it's protected, get back to water. Product holds up in between. Not every weekend."
4. **Complete confidence** — "Know without Googling that what I'm applying won't strip or fade. Trust it completely."
5. **Maximum value preserved** — "When I sell, buyer sees a vessel that's been LOVED. Full asking price. Clearly well cared for."

### Post-Transformation Soundbites:

- **Wife:** "I don't know what you've been doing, but that boat looks better than the day we bought it. Whatever that stuff is — keep using it."
- **Son:** "Dad, this is CLEAN. How are you getting the hull to look like that? Looks brand new. You should tell the guys in the boat group."
- **Marina neighbor:** "Did you have someone come out? This looks like a professional job. Seriously impressive."
- **Dave (glowing, casual):** "Nope. Did it myself. One product. Took about 40 minutes."

## MARKET SPECIFICS

### What Does Success Hinge On?
Boat owners believe they need a product specifically engineered for marine surfaces — not adapted from automotive. They've been burned by "universal" products. They're stuck in trial-and-error. They believe the right product EXISTS somewhere. When they find it? Loyal for life.

### What Must They Give Up to Solve This?
- The excuse to avoid hosting
- The "I've been too busy" story
- The right to complain (guy-bonding topic)
- The self-pity narrative
- The victim identity of "why is this so hard?"

### Who Do They Blame?
- **The Marine Industry** — "They DESIGN it to need 12 products so you spend more money."
- **The Elements** — "UV, saltwater, algae destroy everything."
- **Products That Failed** — "Bought something rated #1, left streaks, spent $60 and two hours and made it WORSE."
- **The Dealer** — "Nobody tells you how to maintain when you buy. You're completely on your own."
- **Themselves** (quietly) — "I let it go. Kept saying next weekend. Now it's two seasons of neglect."

## CURRENT MESSAGING ANALYSIS

**What's working:**
- PROOF block strongest — heritage, manufacturing, global trust, industry demand
- PROMISE has direction — "beautiful all the time," "long-term protection"
- Craftsmanship language repeats: "each and every," "complete line," "surface care and maintenance"

**What needs work:**
- **PAIN implied, not spoken** — never directly addresses Dave's fear, frustration, urgency
- **No constraints addressed** — price skepticism, multi-product hassle never acknowledged
- **Mechanism is weakest** — no secret, no unique angle, no WHY Seapower is better
- **Promise too generic** — "beautiful" and "long-term" need specificity and measurable proof
