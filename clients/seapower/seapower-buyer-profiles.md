# Seapower Buyer Profiles

Related: [[The Script]] [[Memory Loop]]

## BUYER 1: "CAPTAIN DAVE" (Established Owners)

**Demographics:** 48–62, primarily male, coastal/lake communities (FL, TX, MI, NC, CA, TN, MN, SC). $85K–$225K+. Business owner, contractor, manager, skilled tradesperson, retired professional. Married with kids or empty nesters.

**Core Identity:** "I'm not just a boat owner. I'm a captain. This boat is a reflection of who I am."

**Boat Type:** Pontoon, center console, bass boat, cabin cruiser, ski/wakeboard, sport fishing vessel.

**The Story:** Dave worked hard for this boat. It's a trophy representing decades of sacrifice — $28K pontoon to $140K center console. His peers notice it. His family bonds on it. But maintenance is overwhelming and embarrassing.

---

## DAVE'S TOP 5 EMOTIONS

1. **Frustration** — "I've spent $500 on cleaners and my gel coat still looks like hell. What am I doing wrong?"
2. **Wounded Pride** — "That boat is supposed to turn heads. When it pulls into the marina looking dull, that's on ME."
3. **Financial Anxiety** — "Every season it looks worse. I'm watching a six-figure investment depreciate in real time."
4. **Resentment** — "I bought this to enjoy life, not spend every Sunday scrubbing with six products."
5. **Quiet Inadequacy** — "That guy in slip 14 has a boat worth half of mine and it always looks twice as good."

---

## DAVE'S TOP 5 FEARS

1. **Irreversible Damage** — "What if oxidation is already too far gone? What if I've permanently damaged the gel coat?"
2. **Judged at the Marina** — "Everyone notices. A neglected boat tells people you don't have your act together."
3. **Losing Resale Value** — "Buyers hammer you on cosmetics first. I could be leaving $15K–$20K on the table."
4. **Using Wrong Product / Making It Worse** — "I made one mistake on the vinyl. Now I'm scared to touch anything."
5. **Loss of Identity** — "This boat is proof that I made it. When it looks bad, I feel like I'm losing control."

---

## DAVE'S WHAT'S WORKED / WHAT'S FAILED

**Tried:**
- Cabinet full of single-surface bottles → "Can't remember which is which"
- Big-box products (Star Brite, Meguiar's) → "Takes forever, results don't last"
- Professional detailer ($450) → "Looked great a month, can't justify monthly"
- YouTube DIY → "Two hours research, $200 equipment, still not happy"
- Boating forums → "40 different answers, no consensus"

**Won't do:**
- Spend weekends cleaning instead of boating
- Buy another shelf of specialized products
- Pay professional every month
- Risk damaging surfaces experimenting
- Become a marine detailing expert

---

## DAVE'S MAGIC TRANSFORMATION

1. **One product. Every surface. Done.** — "Grab it, run it across everything, looks showroom fresh."
2. **Compliments become routine again** — "I want the 'nice boat' comments back."
3. **Protection that actually lasts** — "Clean it right, know it's protected, get back to water."
4. **Complete confidence** — "Know without Googling it's safe and won't strip or fade."
5. **Maximum value preserved** — "When I sell, buyer sees a vessel that's been LOVED. Full asking price."

---

## DAVE'S DEEPEST OBJECTIONS

1. "All-in-one products never work as well as dedicated ones" → Overcome with: specific proof, before/afters, real testimonials
2. "This is just another product that looks good on label" → Overcome with: years in business, brand reputation, marine heritage, authentic reviews
3. "Is this even safe for MY surfaces?" → Overcome with: explicit confirmation for fiberglass/vinyl/metal combo
4. "Probably just as expensive when you add it all up" → Overcome with: show cost equation of multiple products + detailer costs + resale loss
5. "Never heard of this brand before" → Overcome with: years in industry, vessels serviced, marina partnerships, real boater results

## BUYER 2: "COASTAL COLE" (Next-Generation)

**Demographics:** 24–38, gender-balanced, Sun Belt metros (Miami, Tampa, Austin, Nashville, San Diego, Charlotte, Atlanta, Phoenix). $45K–$95K income, dual-income or hustle-culture earner. Skilled trades, real estate, sales, creative, social media, fitness, hospitality, entrepreneurship.

**Core Identity:** "I'm not just buying a product. I'm buying into an identity. If it doesn't look good and doesn't represent who I am — I'm not interested."

**Watercraft Type:** Jet ski (Sea-Doo, WaveRunner), entry-level pontoon, used runabout, wakeboard boat, inflatable dock.

**Social Profile:** Heavy Instagram/TikTok presence. Life is curated. Gear is intentional. Aesthetic is everything.

**The Tension:** Cole wants his watercraft to photograph like a magazine shoot every weekend. But he doesn't know maintenance and won't spend hours learning. Needs: one-product solution from a brand that looks like it belongs in his world — not his grandfather's garage.

---

## COLE'S TOP 5 EMOTIONS

1. **Image Anxiety** — "Every post gets scanned for water spots. Everyone notices what I didn't."
2. **Rookie Insecurity** — "Everyone seems to know what they're doing. I'm Googling in the parking lot like an idiot."
3. **Fear of Wasting Money** — "I stretched hard to get this. Can't afford to screw it up."
4. **Brand Identity FOMO** — "Where's the brand that actually gets my generation?"
5. **Social Currency Pressure** — "My whole crew watches what I post. My reputation is visible."

---

## COLE'S TOP 5 FEARS

1. **Looking Like a Poser** — "If my ski looks beat at the ramp, they'll know I have no idea what I'm doing. I'm a fraud."
2. **Accidentally Wrecking It** — "What if I use the wrong cleaner and strip the finish? I can't afford to fix that."
3. **Using "Boomer Products"** — "I'm not showing up with something that looks like it's from 1997. The product says something about me too."
4. **Rapid Depreciation** — "I see skis on Facebook Marketplace completely destroyed. That was someone's $12K machine. I'm not ending up like that."
5. **Locked Out of Real Water Culture** — "There's a community I want to belong to. But I feel like an outsider trying to fake my way in."

---

## COLE'S WHAT'S WORKED / WHAT'S FAILED

**Tried:**
- Whatever was at Walmart/Amazon "Marine" section → "Left a weird haze on the plastic"
- TikTok cleaning hacks (Dawn dish soap + pressure washer) → "Looked okay a day then worse"
- Auto detailing products → "Not meant for fiberglass, UV protection is totally different"
- Reddit/Facebook groups → "Half aggressive, half arguing, zero helpful info"
- Nothing — just water rinse → "Now seat is faded and hull has film"

**Won't do:**
- Spend Saturdays detailed cleaning instead of being ON the water
- Buy products that look embarrassing (industrial-looking)
- Invest hours becoming a maintenance expert
- Risk damaging surfaces experimenting
- Support a brand that doesn't reflect who he is

---

## COLE'S MAGIC TRANSFORMATION

1. **Watercraft photographs like magazine shoot every time** — "Pull up, drop the GoPro angle, it looks CLEAN. People ask what my setup is."
2. **One product he trusts completely** — "No fear, no guessing. One bottle, done."
3. **Brand identity he can wear** — "Hoodie at the ramp AND brunch Sunday. Logo looks intentional, like a tattoo sleeve."
4. **Instant credibility in water culture** — "From rookie to authority. People ask ME for advice."
5. **Full value retention** — "Three years later, get top dollar. Buyer knows it's been treated right."

---

## COLE'S DEEPEST OBJECTIONS

1. "Never heard of Sea Power. Is it legit or just good branding?" → Overcome with: real before/afters, authentic testimonials from people like him, marine heritage in new visual language
2. "Can one product really clean everything?" → Overcome with: surface-by-surface TikTok demos (ABS plastic, vinyl specifics)
3. "Is the apparel actually quality or just logo on cheap blank?" → Overcome with: prove quality — they'll feel the fabric and decide in seconds
4. "Is this brand actually part of the culture or trying too hard?" → Overcome with: authenticity over aspiration, credible creators, genuine water culture partnerships
5. "What if the apparel isn't cool when I wear it?" → Overcome with: strong social proof, seeded through on-trend water lifestyle influencers, feel like a discovery not marketing push
