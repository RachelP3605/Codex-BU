## Who they are
Rachel's own wellness e-commerce brand. Curated marketplace for clean wellness and beauty products — supplements, skincare, household, saunas/infrared. Also operates as "The Longevity Lab." Sells direct-to-consumer via Shopify (theswellscore.com).

**Target customer:** Health-conscious consumers frustrated by misleading wellness marketing. Want ingredient transparency, safety, and expert curation. Concerned about toxins, purity, and being misled.

**Core positioning:** "Taking the Guesswork and Risk Out of Wellness & Beauty." Products rigorously reviewed for safety and ingredient purity. Third-party tested supplements. Beauty products screened for toxins (no phthalates, parabens, etc.).

**Business model:** Membership — $59/year (normally $99). Members save 15–25% on every purchase. No reliance on sale events.

## Buyer Profile — "Sarah", age 42
College-educated professional, upper-middle income, married with 2 kids. Considers herself a conscious consumer. Active on social media, follows wellness influencers.

**Core problem:** Overwhelming anxiety and frustration trying to find truly safe wellness products in a market full of deceptive marketing and hidden toxic ingredients.

**Top emotions:**
- Betrayal by brands she trusted that turned out to be lying
- Overwhelm from endless ingredient research
- Guilt about possibly exposing her family to harmful products
- Anger at companies prioritizing profits over health
- Anxiety about long-term toxic exposure

**Top fears:**
- "What if the products I'm using are slowly poisoning my family?"
- "What if my kids develop health issues from all these chemicals?"
- "What if I'm wasting money on fake 'natural' products?"
- "What if I'm being duped by clever marketing again?"
- "What if I can't trust any company's claims?"

**What she's tried and failed:**
- EWG ingredient research (still got fooled)
- DIY natural products (didn't perform)
- Following clean beauty influencers (many were paid/lying)
- Natural food stores (still sell questionable products)
- Amazon "natural" searches (can't trust reviews)

**What she actually wants:**
- Someone else to carry the burden of verification — with real labs, real testing, real science
- Complete confidence in product safety without hours of research
- Permission to trust again without feeling naive
- Peace of mind about her family's health

**The unfiltered truth (from her 2AM confessions):**
- She's not just protecting her family — she's trying to control an uncontrollable world
- The hypervigilance is rooted in fear: her mom's cancer, "environmental factors"
- She's passed her anxiety to her kids and knows it (daughter asked if shampoo could make her sick — at age 8)
- She misses being able to trust. Some nights she deliberately doesn't check ingredients "just to feel normal"
- She wants a scientist, a lab, someone who tests and has nothing to sell her

**Her 3AM search history tells the real story:**
- Is anyone actually testing these ingredients?
- How to know who to trust
- How to stop obsessing about toxins
- Am I crazy about clean products
- Final search: "how to protect family from everything"

**What she blames:**
- FDA for weak regulations
- Companies for deceptive marketing
- Influencers for false claims
- Herself for not researching more

**Copy implications:**
- Do NOT lead with fear — she already has enough. Lead with relief.
- The sale is trust, not product. She's buying "someone finally did the work for me."
- Never use vague "clean" or "natural" language — that's what burned her. Be specific.
- Membership framing: she's not buying products, she's buying a trusted curator.
- Speak to the exhaustion of vigilance, not the danger of toxins.
