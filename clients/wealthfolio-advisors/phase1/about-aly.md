## The Retirement Transition Window

Aly works with individuals between ages 59½ and 70 — the period when most significant retirement transition decisions are made and, in many cases, become permanent.

This period — which extends through age 73, when required minimum distributions begin — represents the highest concentration of consequential, time-sensitive financial decisions most people will ever face. Decisions made during this window include pension elections, Social Security timing, 401(k) rollover choices, Roth conversion strategy, Medicare enrollment, and income sequencing.

Many of these decisions cannot be revisited once made. That is why the transition period itself — not just the years of accumulation that preceded it — warrants dedicated attention.

## Why Retirement Transition Requires Specialized Attention

The accumulation phase of financial planning — saving, investing, building a balance — operates on different principles than the distribution phase. The skills and decisions that build a retirement account are largely distinct from the decisions required to convert that account into income that lasts.

During the transition phase:

- **Pension elections** are typically irrevocable — the option selected at retirement cannot be changed later. [Understanding pension distribution decisions →](/pension-distribution-decisions/)
- **Social Security timing** is permanent — once benefits begin, the rate is set, subject only to annual cost-of-living adjustments. [Social Security timing considerations →](/retirement-income-decisions/)
- **Roth conversion timing** has a meaningful window — the years between retirement and age 73, before required minimum distributions begin, are often cited as a planning opportunity. [Roth conversion factors to consider →](/roth-conversion-considerations/)
- **Withdrawal sequencing** affects lifetime taxes — the order in which money is drawn from taxable, tax-deferred, and tax-free accounts has compounding consequences over a 20 to 30 year period. [Retirement income sequencing →](/retirement-income-decisions/)
- **Mistakes during this period** are difficult to correct and often permanent. [Common retirement transition mistakes →](/retirement-transition-mistakes/)

### How is retirement transition planning different from general financial planning?

Retirement transition planning focuses specifically on the distribution phase — the period when accumulated savings are converted into income. The decisions involved during this phase are largely distinct from accumulation-phase decisions, and many carry permanent consequences. Pension elections cannot typically be changed. General financial planning covers a broader range of life stages; retirement transition planning addresses the specific set of decisions concentrated in the years around retirement.
