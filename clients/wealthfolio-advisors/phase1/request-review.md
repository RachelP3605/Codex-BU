# Take the First Step: Request Your Retirement Transition Review

A Retirement Transition Review is an educational conversation — not a sales call. It is designed to help individuals between ages 55 and 70 better understand the retirement decisions they are facing. There is no cost, no obligation, and no advisory relationship is established. WealthFolio Advisors, LLC, based in Long Beach, California, offers these reviews as a free educational resource for people approaching or recently entering retirement.

## Who May Benefit from a Retirement Transition Review

This review may be useful if you are in any of the following situations:

- You are within five years of retirement and have not yet mapped out the decisions you will face
- You have a pension and are approaching the point where you will need to choose between a lump sum and monthly income
- You have one or more old 401(k) accounts and are uncertain about your options — including whether to roll over, leave in place, or consolidate ([employer retirement plan resources →](/employer-resources/))
- You are approaching a Social Security milestone age and have questions about timing
- You have heard about Roth conversions and want to understand whether they are relevant to your situation
- You retired recently and are working through the transition decisions you are now facing

---

## Topics That May Be Discussed

The review covers educational topics related to retirement transition decisions. Topics may include:

- Retirement income planning — which accounts to draw from and in what order
- 401(k) and IRA rollover options and considerations
- Pension lump sum versus monthly income factors ([pension distribution decisions →](/pension-distribution-decisions/))
- Roth conversion timing and tax considerations ([Roth conversion factors to consider →](/roth-conversion-considerations/))
- Social Security timing — from age 62 to 70 — and the factors that affect that decision
- Tax considerations related to withdrawal sequencing and bracket management
- Required minimum distributions — when they begin, how they are calculated, and how they interact with other income
- Estate planning considerations, including beneficiary designations

These are educational topics for discussion. The review does not constitute individualized investment, tax, accounting, or legal advice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What happens after I submit a request?

A member of the WealthFolio Advisors team will contact you to schedule a time for the review. The conversation is educational and informational. There is no commitment required to continue, no product pitch, and no obligation to take any action as a result of the conversation.

### Is there a cost for the Retirement Transition Review?

No. The Retirement Transition Review is offered at no cost. It is an educational resource for individuals approaching or recently entering retirement. No purchase or commitment is required.

### Does requesting a review mean I'm becoming a client?

No. Requesting or completing a Retirement Transition Review does not establish an advisory relationship with WealthFolio Advisors, LLC. The review is an educational conversation. If you are interested in exploring an advisory relationship after the review, that is a separate process.

### How long does the review take?

The Retirement Transition Review is typically approximately 30 to 45 minutes. The conversation is structured to cover the key retirement decisions relevant to your stage of retirement planning and to give you an opportunity to ask questions in an educational setting.

### What should I prepare before the review?

There is no required preparation. You may find it helpful to have a general sense of your retirement accounts, anticipated retirement date, and any specific questions you have in mind. The review is conversational — there are no forms to complete in advance and no documents required.

### Who conducts the review?

The review is conducted by Aly McFarlane or a member of the WealthFolio Advisors team.
