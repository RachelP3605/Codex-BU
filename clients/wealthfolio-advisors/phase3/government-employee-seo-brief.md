Searchers using "government employee retirement planning" span two groups: federal employees (FERS, TSP, Social Security) and state/local government employees who have pension and deferred savings plans but different structures. The dominant intent is decision research — these are employees within 2–5 years of retirement actively trying to understand their options, particularly around TSP decisions and the FERS survivor benefit election. A secondary intent group is employees who recently retired and are now trying to understand their TSP distribution options or whether they should have moved their TSP to an IRA.

## 9. Direct Answer Seed (40–75 words)

Government employees — particularly federal employees under FERS — often retire with three distinct income sources: a pension, TSP savings, and Social Security. Each of these has its own timing decisions, tax treatment, and interaction with the others. The decisions made around the TSP distribution, FERS survivor benefit election, and Social Security filing date are among the most consequential — and most permanent — a federal employee will make at retirement.

---

## 10. Key Takeaways (3–6 bullets)

- Federal employees under FERS typically retire with three income sources — FERS pension, TSP, and Social Security — each with distinct timing and tax decisions
- The TSP is not required to be moved at retirement — it has its own distribution options worth evaluating before any rollover decision is made
- The FERS survivor benefit election is typically irrevocable — the cost and the protection it provides both deserve careful analysis before the decision is submitted
- WEP and GPO can materially reduce Social Security benefits for government employees with mixed employment history — this should be identified before building an income plan
- Roth TSP is subject to required minimum distributions; Roth IRA is not — this distinction matters for long-term tax planning
- State and local government employees face similar layered decisions, though plan structures differ from the federal FERS/TSP model

## 16. Content Angle / Positioning Note

Government employees are often told their retirement is "safe" because they have a pension. This page challenges that framing — not to create anxiety, but to surface the real decisions that still require active planning: TSP distribution, survivor benefit election, Social Security timing, and the WEP/GPO complications that catch many government employees off guard. The angle is: "A pension doesn't mean the decisions are simple — it means the decisions are layered." Aly McFarlane and WealthFolio Advisors position here as advisors who understand the federal retirement system and can help government employees see the full picture before they commit to irrevocable elections.
