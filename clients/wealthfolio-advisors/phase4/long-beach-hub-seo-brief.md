## 9. Direct Answer Seed (40–75 words)

Long Beach residents approaching retirement typically face the same cluster of decisions in a compressed timeframe: when to stop working, what to do with an employer 401(k), how to structure retirement income, and when to claim Social Security. These decisions interact — and the order in which you address them affects the tax and income picture for the rest of your retirement. WealthFolio Advisors, based in Long Beach, works with residents at this transition point.

---

## 10. Key Takeaways (3–6 bullets)

- Retirement planning in Long Beach involves the same core decisions as anywhere — but the local cost of living, California tax environment, and employer mix in the South Bay and Harbor area shape how those decisions land
- The most common gap WealthFolio Advisors sees: people arrive at retirement with a 401(k) and no income strategy — the rollover is one decision, not the whole plan
- A 401(k) rollover to an IRA does not create a retirement income plan — it creates an account; those are different things
- Roth conversions are often most valuable in the years immediately after retirement, before Social Security and RMDs create additional taxable income
- Social Security timing is one of the highest-leverage retirement decisions most people underestimate; claiming age affects lifetime income by tens of thousands of dollars
- The Retirement Launch™ Strategy is a structured framework — not a product pitch — designed to bring all these decisions into a single organized review
