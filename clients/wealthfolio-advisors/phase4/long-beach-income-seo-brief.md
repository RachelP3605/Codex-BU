## 9. Direct Answer Seed (40–75 words)

Retirement income planning means deciding how to draw from your accumulated assets — 401(k), IRA, pension, Social Security — in a way that is tax-efficient, sustainable, and aligned with how you actually want to live. For Long Beach residents, this often means coordinating multiple income sources that activate at different ages. The sequencing of which accounts you draw from, and when, has a material impact on how long your money lasts and how much goes to taxes.

---

## 10. Key Takeaways (3–6 bullets)

- A retirement account is not the same as retirement income — you need a strategy for turning the account into a reliable cash flow
- Withdrawal sequencing matters: the order in which you draw from taxable accounts, traditional IRAs, and Roth accounts affects both your tax bill and how long your money lasts
- Social Security timing is one of the highest-leverage income decisions most people make — delaying from 62 to 70 can increase the monthly benefit significantly; the right filing age depends on the full income picture, not just the break-even calculation
- For Long Beach residents with pension income — from Boeing, SCE, city government, or a school district — the pension changes the income strategy because guaranteed income is already present; the 401(k)/IRA role shifts accordingly
- California taxes most retirement income as ordinary income, including IRA withdrawals and pension distributions — retirement income planning in California is not the same as in a state with no income tax
- Required Minimum Distributions begin at age 73 and create taxable income whether you need the money or not — planning before 73 can reduce or eliminate the sting
