## 9. Direct Answer Seed (40–75 words)

When you leave an employer in Long Beach — or retire — you typically have three options for your 401(k): leave it in the plan, roll it to an IRA, or roll it to a new employer's plan. There is no single right answer. The decision depends on your age, income needs, investment preferences, and what you plan to do with the money. A rollover is not a retirement plan — it is one decision within a larger plan.

---

## 10. Key Takeaways (3–6 bullets)

- A 401(k) rollover is not the same as a retirement income plan — rolling over the account creates an IRA; it does not create a strategy for how to use the money
- You have options beyond rolling to an IRA: leaving the balance in the former employer's plan is sometimes the right call, especially if the plan has low-cost investment options or if you are between 55 and 59½
- Direct rollovers (plan-to-plan or plan-to-IRA) avoid the 20% mandatory withholding that applies to indirect rollovers — the method matters
- California taxes IRA withdrawals as ordinary income — the rollover itself is not a taxable event, but future distributions will be taxed by both the IRS and California
- Rolling over before you have an income strategy often leads to the wrong decisions about when and how to withdraw — sequencing matters
- Many Long Beach-area workers have both a pension and a 401(k) — the rollover decision should be evaluated alongside the pension election, not in isolation

## 16. Content Angle / Positioning Note

This page is not a rollover how-to from a brokerage that wants to receive the assets. It is a decision education page from a Long Beach-based advisor who has helped workers in this region understand their rollover options for decades. The angle is: "A rollover is one decision, not a plan — and the most common mistake is treating it as though it is." The page earns its authority by being clear about what a rollover does and does not accomplish, explaining the mechanics plainly, and pointing the reader toward the next decision rather than just completing the current one. WealthFolio Advisors' local presence reinforces that this guidance is coming from someone in the same community, not a national platform indifferent to whether the decision serves the person or the platform.
