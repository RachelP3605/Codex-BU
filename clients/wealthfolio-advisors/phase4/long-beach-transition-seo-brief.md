## 9. Direct Answer Seed (40–75 words)

Retirement transition planning means identifying and sequencing the decisions that determine how retirement actually works financially. For most Long Beach residents approaching retirement, that means: deciding when to stop working, understanding what happens to the employer 401(k), evaluating pension options if applicable, figuring out how to generate income, and choosing a Social Security filing strategy. These decisions connect — making one without considering the others is the most common planning mistake.

---

## 10. Key Takeaways (3–6 bullets)

- Retirement transition planning is not the same as retirement income planning — the transition is about the sequence of decisions, the income plan is about what happens after
- Most people approaching retirement have more than one major financial decision in front of them simultaneously — they interact, and the order matters
- Long Beach residents who worked for Boeing, Southern California Edison, the Port of Long Beach, or the City of Long Beach often face pension elections in addition to 401(k) decisions — two decisions, not one
- The period between age 59½ and 70 is the most consequential planning window — decisions made here set the structure for the rest of retirement
- A financial review before retirement is different from an ongoing financial planning relationship — many people need the former even if they have never engaged the latter
- The Retirement Launch™ Strategy is a structured way to work through these decisions — not a product, not a sales process
