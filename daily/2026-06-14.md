## Current state

Rachel is evaluating Sound Resource Guide, a curated resource site for music creators to find tools, platforms, services, events, and resources to make, release, and monetize music. The project name is Sound Resource Guide with the tagline: "Find the tools, people, platforms, and resources to make, release, and monetize your music."

The important correction from today: this should not be built as a directory first with SEO as the main hope. The strategy must start as a traffic and offer test. Directory build comes second.

## Decisions made

- Do not rely on SEO alone. That is too slow and likely small potatoes.
- Launch around a sharper offer first: "The Independent Artist Money Map."
- Use the Money Map to test paid/social/newsletter traffic before spending time building a full directory.
- MVP wedge should start with money/release/admin confusion: distribution, PROs, MLC, SoundExchange, publishing admin, metadata, royalty collection, and sync readiness.
- Newsletter should be one tool/resource per week, not five tools per week.
- With Rachel's chosen launch-test budget of $300-$500/month, traffic should be tested through a mix of small paid social, possible Reddit/community-style ads, and small paid newsletter placements.
- DAWs and generic production tools are not the first wedge. They can come later after traffic proof.
- Badges/awards may be useful later for backlinks and vendor sharing, but they should be editorial only and not sold.
- The directory can still use Brilliant Directories as the MVP container, but not before the traffic offer proves there is audience pull.
